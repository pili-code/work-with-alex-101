## What Alex asks — ranked by how often it recurs

### 1. Attribution: "Where did it come from?" (almost every call)
- May 14: "Where does this person come from? Do they come from the job board? Do they come from YouTube? Are we able to do that? That's the goal, right?"
- May 28: "We got 303 visits out of thousands of YouTube views — you're estimating because we can't track precisely?"
- Jan 15: "You can track click-through rate... it means you install a pixel."
- Every lead/signup must have a verifiable source.

### 2. Conversion rate + the funnel math (every call)
- May 14: "Once they're on the page, how many convert? The conversion rate is 1.6%. Figure out the conversion rate that makes us break even."
- May 28: "What's the percentage of users we converted? Where's the bottleneck? Where's the leverage?"
- May 21: "If the conversion rate is 22.2%, we can spend $3.72 per click at 30% gross margin."
- Jun 4: "What's the conversion rate of the community landing page? Total visits to total subscribers."

### 3. "Did you TEST it? Is it actually tracked?" — the trust theme (strongest coaching)
- May 14: "How do you know it's properly set up? Did you test it? Define 'tested.' If you cannot show me that it worked, it's not tested. **Don't tell me it's tested and then it's not — because next time I need to ask you seven questions to trust the answer.**"
- Jun 4: "Google Analytics is installed, right?"
- Sloppy/unverified reporting actively costs credibility with him.

### 4. "Send me numbers" — trends, up or down (Feb 19, Feb 26)
- Feb 19: "Send me some numbers. How many emails did we send? Reply rate — going up or down? How many calls scheduled — up or down?"
- Feb 26: "Are we getting better or not? We don't have a spreadsheet report? I only want to talk about what can be better."
- He wants deltas and trendlines, not vanity absolutes.

### 5. Conclusion-first / "where's the leverage" / focus (every call)
- Jun 4: "Just give us the conclusion so we can talk about what matters."
- May 28: "When you do this report, the useful thing is where is leverage. Where's the bottleneck?"
- Feb 26: "Focus on what matters — nice-to-have vs must-have. The awareness of the numbers is a must-have."
- Feb 19: "Be more structured and concise; focus on key business results."

### 6. Unit economics: CAC, break-even, margin, revenue quality
- May 14: full CAC/break-even model live on screen — "I spend a thousand, I make a thousand, even at break-even it's profitable because..."
- May 21: "$1,700 with zero CAC. Every additional person costs nothing. 1,000 people = $170,000 revenue."
- Apr 23: "Revenue that comes with work is worse quality than revenue that comes without. Revenue per employee."

### 7. From data to action: "Why aren't they converting? What do we DO?"
- May 28: "The actions are: how do we convert more? Why aren't people converting? Can we get that info? How do we get more people to click?"

### 8. Strategic zoom-out: "What are we trying to achieve? Where is TDP going?"
- Apr 23: "What's our main goal with YouTube? Where do we see TDP going — keep doing consulting, or something bigger?"

### 9. Bias to action: test it, set it as a goal, kill it if it fails
- May 21: "Why decide up front it'll fail? We test, see what happens, we can always kill it. I'm 60% confident. Let's make it a goal so we push."

## Agenda improvements (mapped to the patterns above)
1. **Bottom Line box at top** — what changed, the one number, the one decision needed (#5).
2. **Conversion rate at every funnel step** (visits → clicks → signups → customers), each with % (#2).
3. **Source column on every lead/signup** — YouTube / LinkedIn / job board / referral (#1).
4. **"Tracking confirmed? Y/N + how I tested it"** next to every metric (#3).
5. **Trend arrows (▲/▼ vs last week)** on each KPI, not just absolutes (#4).
6. **Unit-economics line** where relevant: CAC, break-even CR, margin (#6).
7. **For each finding, a proposed action** — "why aren't they converting → here's what I'll do" (#7).
8. **Tag items Decision / FYI / Blocked**; spend time on Decision + Blocked only (#5).
9. Cut process notes; lead with the outcome (#5).

## The single most important coaching quote
> "Don't tell me it's tested and then it's not. Because next time you tell me something, I need to
> ask you seven questions to trust the answer." — May 14

Reliability of your reporting is the currency. One verified number beats five estimated ones.
